We had the biggest snow storm in 10 years here in the Eastern U.S. The days leading up to the storm were so exciting. Stores were busy, bustling with people stocking up on groceries. Businesses were anticipating being closed for the weekend, cars were covering their windshields with cardboard and sheets of wood. As the storm approached, the temperature dropped to single digits. We awoke to several inches of snow–and that would've been a lot if it stopped there. The entire day was a blizzard, it was a sight to behold. I left in the afternoon and it turned out to be more of an adventure than I expected. The wind was so strong and the temperature so slow, my face was stinging with the sharp pelting of small, frozen solid snowflakes. That didn't stop the town from enjoying it, though. There were hundreds of people sledding, cross country skiing, strolling, and of course, shoveling. I helped push a stuck minivan out of a snowbank, I saw a snowboarder being pulled through the streets by a Jeep. Our local bar had skis leaned outside, I thought I was in Park City! It was so much fun, but now massive snowbanks line the streets and sidewalks. It's made things difficult to traverse and the weather hasn't warmed up at all. Now I can hardly wait for some warm weather to melt this all away into a memory.
