This has been a good year for reading, although I read 2 books and a little over 1000 pages less than I did in 2024 which surprises me because last year with our move to NYC, I think there was a solid 3 months where I didn't even touch a book. I attribute that, in part, to Swann's Way which was very dense and slow moving. Still, almost everything I read left a big impression on me this year. I discovered Steinbeck and absolutely loved The Winter of Our Discontent. It was so familiar and cozy, it reminded me of watching old movies. I was enthralled by the City & the City by Miéville and crushed that book in just a few days. I read some great classics like The Picture of Dorian Gray, Swann's Way, and Wuthering Heights which all left lasting impressions that I still think about everyday. The only books I didn't feel as impressed with were the three Auster novels I read, which to me felt repetitive and shallow. Not bad, but not great. The Communist Manifesto, while I ranked last in context with the other things I read this year, was still something I was happy to have read and am glad to understand better. This coming year I hope to discover more contemporary authors and to finish the My Struggle books by Knausgaard.
